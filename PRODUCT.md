# Product

## Register

product

## Users
People who want to identify bug bites quickly and with minimal friction. They are usually looking for fast clarity, practical guidance, and enough confidence to decide whether to monitor the bite, treat it at home, or seek medical help.

## Product Purpose
BiteGuard helps people detect likely bug bites in a fast and easy way. The product should support quick scan-based identification, clear educational guidance, lightweight triage support, and useful scan history so people can feel more informed and less overwhelmed.

Success means users can move from uncertainty to action quickly: they can understand what the bite may be, what to do next, what warning signs to watch for, and revisit prior scans when needed.

## Brand Personality
Professional, creative, approachable.

The product should feel medically responsible without becoming cold or sterile. It should feel thoughtfully designed, calm under pressure, and easy to understand for non-experts.

## Anti-references
Anything that feels like AI vibecoded slop.

Avoid generic glassy AI app templates, empty “smart assistant” theater, overproduced gradients with no purpose, vague medical futurism, and interfaces that feel more like a demo than a trustworthy tool.

## Design Principles
1. Reduce panic through clarity: the interface should turn uncertainty into understandable next steps.
2. Move quickly without feeling rushed: users should get answers fast, but the product should still feel careful and trustworthy.
3. Keep expertise approachable: guidance should feel informed and responsible without sounding clinical or intimidating.
4. Make design carry meaning: every visual choice should support trust, comprehension, and usability rather than trend-chasing.
5. Reward return use: scan history and repeated interactions should feel organized, lightweight, and worth coming back to.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA as a baseline.

Use clear hierarchy, readable typography, strong enough contrast, and touch-friendly controls. Motion should feel good and thoughtful, but never become distracting or essential to understanding. Reduced-motion users should still get a complete and polished experience.
